Ushuaia The End of the World!

Ushuaia is a town located by the southernmost tip of Argentina (the reason for its nickname: The End of the World), this city marks all of the end of South America and serves as a port of call for Antarctic cruises. It has unique landscapes of mountains, sea, glaciers, and woods near the famous park Tierra del Fuego. This place has been established as a penal colony by the 20th century and is currently a popular jumping-off point for trips to Antarctica. Some of its more popular places are San Juan de Salvamento Lighthouse (known as the End of the World Lighthouse) on the Isla de los Estados, and the End of the World Museum. Here, you'll find exhibits relating to the region's natural history, aboriginal life, and early penal colonies. It is also a really important place for being an administrative center, a light industrial port, and a tourist hub. Thanks to being the most southernmost city in the world, Argentina holds that title which makes it really important for the country. This is just a really attractive place for tourists since its scenic waterfalls, coastal forests, glaciers, mountains, and spectacular wildlife. This place has just tons of attractions and activities for people to do and explore throughout the town which makes it much more gorgeous and lovely.

Most beautiful place to see species!

The blue deep waters make it very easy for it to be one of the most popular cruise destinations in Argentina.With over 2,000 southern right whales, the Whale Conservation Institute and the Ocean Alliance, Peninsula Valdés has the largest population of these whales. The place is home to six nature reserves and is one of the world's most important whale-watching destinations, particularly around Puerto Pirámides and the city of Puerto Madryn. You can also find killer whales, dolphins, penguins, sea elephants, and a wide variety of birds have been spotted. The Península Valdés is very important to Argentina because it is home to many wildlife species like the ones we have talked about, it is also important because it is a site of global significance for the conservation of marine mammals. The waters in these places are scientifically beautiful. They are one of the best places in South America to see the species up close and also the waters. It's very likely to see the whales from the beach, with hundreds of them coming into the bays.

Home to the longest road!

Argentina is home to the famous Ruta 40 (Route 40), one of the world's longest roads. Ruta 40 begins in the extreme south of Argentina and travels for over 5,000 kilometers, passing through Argentine Patagonia, in that road the Route of the Seven Lakes, it is a 108-kilometer section of Ruta 40. The Route of the Seven Lakes is a winding lake-and-mountain road in Argentina's Lakes District that connects the lakeside towns of Villa La Angostura and San Martín de los Andes.This landscape is a seventh heaven for outdoor adventurers, there are so many things to see like, many villages and natural highlights to visit along this road.This is very important because it's the most impressive route in the country and the first choice for anyone who would like to venture into a 180-kilometer self-drive trip experience. Tourists can experience multiple different adventures like canoeing, camping and watching the spectacular views, this place has it all.

The highest mountain in the southern hemisphere!

Aconcagua is the highest mountain in the southern hemisphere, it is actually impressive. The top of the mountain has a height of 6,961 meters and is part of the Andes, close to the border with Chile. For climbers, Aconcagua is a big challenge , as it's one of the Seven Summits, the ultimate climbing list that includes the highest mountains in each continent. Aconcagua is the highest peak outside of Asia..Aconcagua Provincial Park, a protected area that offers a number of activities for outdoor lovers, including hiking and skiing. For those who are up for something a bit more challenging, the Horcones-Confluencia trail takes hikers up to the basecamp on the route up to the top. It's a 14-kilometer-long trail with an elevation of 538 meters.It is believed to have the highest death rate of any mountain in South America around three a year which has earned it the nickname "Mountain of Death". More than 100 people have died on Aconcagua since records began.

The Beautiful Colors!

Quebrada de Humahuaca is a narrow mountain valley in northern Argentina. It is famous for its spectacular rock formations and hills, as well as its indigenous Quechuan villages. The rocky, multicolored slopes of Seven Colors Hill rise above the Spanish colonial village of Purmamarca in the south. Quebrada (which literally translates to "broken") is a name used in Argentina to refer to deep ravines. And the 155 kilometer long Quebrada de Humahuaca in the northwest of the country is a perfect example of a beautiful ravine with a wild river running through it in summer (the river dries up in winter).The Quebrada de Humahuaca valley has been used over the past 10,000 years as a crucial passage for the transport of people and ideas from the high Andean lands to the plains. The hikes around the Río Grande are stunning for the tourist, the town plaza, and going up to the Monumento de la Independencia will be a good challenge. If youre here during one of the towns many festivals, Carnival, expect to see dancing, music, masks, and costumes in the streets during these local celebrations.
